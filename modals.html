Professor Guillermo Aguilar received his Bachelors degree in Mechanical and Electrical Engineering from the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM) in 1993, and his Masters and Doctor of Philosophy degrees in Mechanical Engineering from the University of California, Santa Barbara in 1995 and 1999, respectively. His research focuses on laser-tissue interactions, thermo-mechanical response of human tissue, laser-assisted cryosurgery, and spray/droplet deposition and heat transfer. His most recent interest concentrate around the implementation and laser processing of biocompatible materials for a variety of biomedical applications.

Javier E. Garay received his B.S. in Mechanical Engineering (1999), M.S. in Materials Science and Engineering (2002) and Ph.D. in Materials Science and Engineering (2004) all from the University of California, Davis. He joined the Jacobs School of Engineering at UC San Diego in 2015. Prior to that he was a professor of Materials Science & Engineering and Mechanical Engineering at UC Riverside where he also served as Chair of the Materials Science & Engineering Program from 2012-2015. Professor Garay's research focuses on advanced material processing and synthesis with particular emphasis on designing the micro/nanostructure of bulk materials for property optimization.

Dr. Masaru Rao received his B.S. in Material Science and Engineering from the University of Florida and his Ph.D. in Materials Engineering from the University of California, Santa Barbara. He is a member of ASME, IEEE Electron Devices and Engineering in Medicine & Biology Societies, and MRS. Dr. Rao's research interests lie in the development and application of novel micro/nanofabrication methods and materials for MEMS, microfluidics, and biomedical microdevices.

Dr. Huinan Liu received her B.S. in Materials Science and Engineering from the University of Science and Technology in Beijing, M.S. in Materials Science and Engineering from Purdue University, and Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering from Brown University. Her research focuses on biomaterials guided tissue regeneration, nanotechnology, ceramic nanoparticles, degradable polymers, nanocomposites, resorbable metals, surface treatment for medical implants and devices, controlled drug delivery, and stem cell therapy.

Dr. Lorenzo Mangolini received his Ph.D. and M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Minnesota, Minneapolis, and received his B.S. from the Polytechnic University of Milan, Italy. His research focuses on the development of devices based on nanostructured materials for the solution of energy-related issues, characterization of nano-materials, Plasma Enhanced Chemical Vapor Deposition (PECVD) synthesis of nanostructures and semiconductor quantum dots, and advanced process characterization and modeling of gas-phase reactive systems.

Dr. Devin Binder received his M.D. and Ph.D. degrees from Duke University and then undertook an internship in general surgery and a residency in neurological surgery at the University of California, San Francisco. He completed a neurosurgical and neuroscience fellowship at the University of Bonn, as a William P. Van Wagenen Fellow in Germany. His research focuses on applying new discoveries from the basic sciences of glial-neuronal interactions and neurophotonics to create new ways to diagnose and treat neurological diseases, including seizures, traumatic brain injury and spinal cord injury, and cerebral and spinal edema.

Dr. Santiago Camacho-Lopez received both his B.Sc. (1992) and M.Sc. (1994) degrees in Physics from Universidad Autonoma Metropolitana-Iztapalapa in Mexico City, and his Ph.D. (2000) degree in Physics from Imperial College of Science, Technology and Medicine, University of London, UK. He started to work in the ultrashort pulse lasers field at University of Toronto where he was a postdoctoral fellow (2000-2003). He joined the Department of optics in CICESE, Ensenada, Baja California, Mexico, in 2003. Since then he is a full time researcher and has chaired the Department (2010-2016). His research interests include processing of materials using ultrashort pulse lasers, nonlinear optics, and biophotonics. Dr. Camacho- Lopez holds (2013-present) an Adjunct Professor position in the ME Department at UCR.

Juan Hernández-Cordero received his B.Sc. (electrical engineering, 1992) from the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), followed by a year as a research and assistant at the Materials Research Institute (IIM) of the UNAM. He was awarded a full scholarship to Brown University, where he earned a Master’s and Ph.D. degrees from the Division of Engineering in 1996 and 1998, respectively, followed by a year of Postdoctoral research at the Laboratory for Lightwave Technology in Boston University. He is currently a full time tenured researcher at the Materials Research Institute (IIM) of the UNAM, where he has established the Laboratory for Fiber Lasers and Optical Fiber Sensors. His research interests include optical fiber sensors, fiber lasers and fiber devices, random lasers, and optical fiber traps.

Rubén Ramos-García received his Bachelors degree in Physics and Mathematics from the National Polytechnic Institute in Mexico City (1990), his Masters degree in optics from the Center for Research in Optics in Leon Mexico (1992) and his Doctor of Philosophy in Physics from the Imperial College in London UK (1997). Since 1997 joined the Optics Department at the National Research Center of Astrophysics, Optics and Electronics. His research focuses in biophotonics in particular optical tweezers, optical cavitation and photodynamic therapy.

David Halaney received his B.S. in Biology from Trinity University in 2011, and then spent 4 years as a Research Assistant at the University of Texas Health Science Center at San Antonio, TX, where his research focused on improving light-based diagnosis and treatment of cardiovascular diseases. He joined Dr. Guillermo Aguilar's Laboratory of Transport Phenomena for Biomedical Applications in the UCR Department of Mechanical Engineering in September of 2015, where he is working towards his Ph.D. and acting as a Project Manager for the Window to the Brain development under C-SAMBA.

Aaron F. Cipriano received his B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Cal Poly Pomona in 2007, and then spent 4 years working as an engineer in extraction of hydrocarbons in Guatemala and Belize. He joined Dr. Huinan Liu’s Biomaterials and Nanomedicine Lab at UC Riverside as a Ph.D. student in 2011. His research is centered at the intersection of hard tissue regeneration and materials science and engineering, aiming to maximize the properties of bulk Magnesium alloys and design tools to study these materials in vitro, to develop biodegradable metallic implants for orthopedic applications. Aaron received his M.Sc. in Materials Science and Engineering in 2015 at UC Riverside, and successfully defended his Ph.D. dissertation in February of 2016.

Gottlieb Uahengo Jr. received his B.Sc. in Physics from Augsburg College in Minneapolis, MN and a M.Sc. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of California, Riverside.
