Research

C-SAMBA has 5 on-going grant-funded projects: SPOT, PIRE, EAGER, DARPA, LASCE, and AFOSR. The projects are conducting researching ranging from manufacturing to biocompatability.

SPOT

  • Nanopowder Control bubble and acoustic dynamics of optical cavitation using environmental, optical, and fluid factors
  • Relate roughhness/elasticity of skin to cavitation dynamics
  • Characterize thermal consequences at and within skin-liquid layer boundary
  • Fabricate prottype of optical cavitation-based skin poration device
  • Demonstrate skin poration

PIRE

  • Nanopowder synthesis
  • Implant fabrication: new materials and manufacturing
  • Characterization of ceramics: optical, mechanical, thermal
  • Fiber development and coupling
  • Laser-written phottnic structures
  • Biocompatibility
  • Optical imaging and therapy

EAGER

  • Implant low-temperature aging-comparing optical properties of various grain sizes and Yttria density
  • Changes in optical properties due to fouling
  • Anti-fouling - comparing various grain sizing and Yttri density

DARPA

  • Fabricate transparent ceramics
  • Build sealing rig
  • Perform laser sealing
  • Characterize seal

LASCE

  • Balance heat input vs. cooling enhancement by LASCE
  • Determine sensitivity of fluid flow to laser parameters
  • Investigate physics of multiple cavitation events; effect on cooling

AFOSR

  • Measure peak temperature and temperature gradient during laser-metal interaction
  • Model thermal and electronic effects occuring during laser-induced oxidation
  • Contrast laser-induced oxides with conventional thermally-induced oxides
  • Compare features of laser-induced oxides formed in ctrolled oxygen vs. normal air
  • Demonstrate large-area oxide formation
